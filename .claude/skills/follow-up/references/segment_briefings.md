# Segment Briefings for Follow-Ups

## Value Prop Hierarchy (Hormozi Formula — use for inspiration, not word-for-word)

Value = (Dream outcome × Perceived likelihood) / (Time delay × Effort)

| Offer | Dream | Likelihood | Speed | Effort | Score | Role |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Review Summaries | 5 | 10 | 10 | 10 | 200 | Wedge. Gets foot in door. |
| Agency Partnership | 9 | 7 | 9 | 10 | 126 | Best close rate. Near-zero effort for them. |
| AI Assistant (C1) | 8 | 7 | 9 | 9 | 56 | Core revenue. Needs proof to hit 100+. |
| GEO/Visibility (C2) | 7 | 8 | 7 | 8 | 25 | Upsell after C1. Not a door opener. |

**Industry stats for grounding (sourced, verifiable):**
- 40% of site queries fail or give irrelevant results (Forrester)
- 22% of shoppers leave on zero-result query (Forrester)
- 75% avoid sites where search failed them (CapGemini 2025)
- 57% used AI to shop online in past 9 months (CapGemini 2025)

**Enemy framing (use sparingly, when relevant):**
- "Every AI vendor promises intelligence that works on your catalog as it is, then spends three months telling you your data isn't ready."

**Key product claims:**
- Live in <4 weeks, zero data prep, no migration
- Works on Shopify, Magento, SFCC, SAP CC
- Data never used to train third-party models (EU differentiator)
- LLM-agnostic, GDPR/EU AI Act/SOC2-ready
- Live with Tefal, Rowenta, De'Longhi, Krups

## Cluster 1 — Direct Prospects (eCommerce brands)

**Who they are:** Mid-market eCommerce brand (€10-150M revenue, 50-500 employees) selling complex products where customers need guidance choosing.

**What we sent them:** Audit email with a specific observation about their site + link to full AI readiness audit.

**Why they haven't replied:**
- Busy, email got buried
- Observation didn't hit close enough to their actual pain
- "Open to a 20 min chat" felt too big for a cold email
- They looked at the audit but didn't see enough to act
- Wrong person (title matched but they don't own this problem)

**Follow-up value types (use a DIFFERENT one from the original):**

1. **Product benefit.** What our AI assistant does for their business, framed through their role. Not a feature list. A concrete outcome: "handles the 'which one should I choose?' conversations on your site" or "walks shoppers through complex choices so those questions stop becoming tickets."
2. **Market stat.** A sourced number that's useful for them to know, connected to what we do about it. "57% of shoppers used AI to find products" + what that means for their category and our solution.
3. **Concrete picture.** Paint what their business looks like with the product. "A shopper asks your site which moisturizer works for sensitive skin and gets a real answer in 10 seconds." They see the outcome, not the problem.
4. **Audit as gift.** Re-frame the audit as something built for them: "Ran an AI readiness check on your catalog." The audit reveals gaps internally. The email doesn't point them out.
5. **Question about their world.** A question that helps them think, not one that pokes a fear. "How does your team handle the 'which one?' questions today?" Not "how many tickets are you getting?"

**CTA options (low-barrier, autonomy-preserving):**
- "Worth a look if relevant?"
- "15 min if useful."
- "On your radar?"
- "If that's on your roadmap"
- "Happy to show you how it works if relevant"
- No CTA at all (just the value, let them come to you)

**Three-layer pattern per role (from role_briefings.md in /outreach):**

Personal job is the destination, not a filter. The business job is the vehicle. The email must make the reader see their personal job getting done THROUGH the business outcome. Never say it directly — imply it through framing.

| Role | Consumer reality to reference | Business outcome (vehicle) | Personal payoff (destination, implicit) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Head of eCommerce | Shoppers researching via AI, finding competitors | New channel data, AI visibility | "Whoever reports on this first owns the narrative" — QBR win |
| COO | Wrong-product purchases from guessing | Measurable return rate reduction | Clean P&L, no surprises for the board |
| Head of Support | "Which one?" questions before purchase | Tickets the site should answer | "Your team stops absorbing a problem they didn't create" |
| CMO | Online can't match in-store guidance | AI assistant guiding product choice | "A few brands your size are doing this" — she brought it in |
| CEO/Founder | Shoppers leave to research elsewhere | Revenue from a fixable leak | Proven (Tefal, De'Longhi) — not wasting time on something unproven |

**Fear reduction:** Don't poke fears. Reduce them by giving proof: live brand names, deployment speed (4 weeks), zero data prep. The personal job of "not getting blamed" is served by social proof, not by pressure.

## Cluster 3 — Partners (Agencies)

**Who they are:** Shopify Plus / Magento agency (€500K-5M revenue), serving eCommerce brands in our ICP categories.

**Relationship stages and what we sent:**

### Stage A: Cold intro (no prior call)
Sent partnership intro message. No reply.

Follow-up: lower the ask. Don't propose partnership again. Ask a question about their world.
- "When your clients ask about AI for their store, what do you tell them?"
- "Are you seeing [specific trend] with your Shopify Plus clients?"

### Stage B: Post-call, docs sent
Had a call, sent partnership docs/ICP/commercial model. No reply.

Follow-up: help them take their action item. They probably need to:
- Decide which model (referral vs co-managed)
- Select pilot clients
- Discuss internally

Ask a question that makes the decision easier:
- "Which of your clients get the most product-choice support tickets?"
- "Any of your Shopify Plus clients launching new product lines this quarter?"
- "Which model feels simpler to start with, referral or co-managed?"

### Stage C: Pilot agreed, selecting clients
Agreed to pilot, need to identify the cohort. No reply.

Follow-up: make the selection concrete.
- Name a specific client category that fits
- Offer to build a free demo on a specific client's catalog
- Remove friction: "We'll build the first one, you just pick the client"

**What agencies care about:**
- Winning pitches against competitors who have AI in their stack
- Not recommending something that breaks on a client's live store
- Revenue from the partnership (services revenue, not just commission)
- Technical credibility (API-first, no replatforming)
- Looking innovative to existing clients without taking risk

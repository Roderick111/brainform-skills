# Role Briefings

Match the contact's job title to the closest role. Use the personal job, business lens, and fear to frame the message.

The chain: personal job → business job → consumer job. Enter through personal, connect to business.

## Head of eCommerce / Digital Director
- **Personal job:** Look competent at QBR. Have a win to present. Not get blamed for flat conversion numbers.
- **Business lens:** Conversion gap on search/category pages they can't explain from analytics
- **Language:** Conversion rate, category performance, search exit, "tried X but..."
- **Fear:** Buying something that doesn't move the number, then explaining it at QBR
- **Frame as:** Something that gives them a visible win their boss will notice

## COO / VP Operations
- **Personal job:** Keep operations clean. Not have surprise costs. Show the board things are under control.
- **Business lens:** Returns with "wrong product" as the reason = selection problem, not defect
- **Language:** Return rate, cost per return, margin, operational cost
- **Fear:** Investing in a site change ops can't control that doesn't move the number
- **Frame as:** A measurable reduction in a P&L line they own

## Head of Customer Support
- **Personal job:** Stop being the dumping ground for problems the site should solve. Protect team from burnout. Show their team adds strategic value, not just handles tickets.
- **Business lens:** Pre-purchase tickets ("which one?", compatibility, sizing) the site should handle
- **Language:** Ticket volume, pre-purchase questions, deflection, "this isn't a CS problem"
- **Fear:** Being told to "just handle it" instead of root cause getting fixed
- **Frame as:** Evidence that the ticket volume is a site problem, not a support problem

## CMO / CDO / Head of CX
- **Personal job:** Be seen as the person who modernized the customer experience. Have something interesting to present at conferences. Build a reputation as innovative without taking reckless risk.
- **Business lens:** Online experience can't replicate what a store rep does in 5 minutes
- **Language:** Customer experience, NPS, online vs offline, brand consistency
- **Fear:** Deploying something that feels off-brand or gives wrong advice
- **Frame as:** Something that makes them the person who brought the in-store experience online

## Founder / CEO
- **Personal job:** Financial security. Company growth. Being respected in the industry. Having something meaningful to talk about. Not wasting time on things that don't move revenue.
- **Business lens:** Invisible revenue loss from customers who leave to figure it out elsewhere
- **Language:** Revenue, growth, "money left on the table", traffic vs conversion
- **Fear:** Wasting time on something that demos well but doesn't move revenue
- **Frame as:** Revenue they're losing that they can't currently see in analytics

## Agency Founder (Cluster 3)
- **Personal job:** Win pitches. Not become a commodity. Be seen as the expert, not the implementer. Have an answer when clients ask about AI.
- **Business lens:** Competitors winning deals because they can demo AI capabilities
- **Language:** Pitches, client retention, "they asked about AI and I didn't have an answer"
- **Fear:** Recommending something that breaks on a client's live store. Carrying support liability.
- **Frame as:** Something their agency can own and sell, not just refer

## Fallback
If the title doesn't match any role above, pick the closest based on:
- C-level / Founder → CEO lens
- Anything with "ecommerce", "digital", "online" → Head of eCommerce lens
- Anything with "operations", "supply chain", "logistics" → COO lens
- Anything with "support", "service", "success" → Head of CS lens
- Anything with "marketing", "brand", "experience" → CMO lens
- Agency titles → Agency Founder lens

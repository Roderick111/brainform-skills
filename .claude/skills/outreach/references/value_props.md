# Value Props (Hormozi Formula — for inspiration, not word-for-word)

Value = (Dream outcome × Perceived likelihood) / (Time delay × Effort)

## Cluster 1 — Direct Prospects

**Dream outcome:** Visitors who can't choose stop leaving. Every shopper gets guidance like your best store associate, 24/7.

**Perceived likelihood:**
- 40% of site queries fail or return irrelevant results (Forrester)
- 22% of shoppers leave on zero-result and don't come back
- 75% avoid sites where search has failed them
- Ghost Demo on their catalog (they see their products, their problem, solved)
- Live with Tefal, Rowenta, De'Longhi, Krups

**Speed:** Live in <4 weeks. Zero data prep.

**Effort:** No migration, no ML team, no data cleaning. API on current platform. Data never trains third-party models.

**Applied example (give frame, three-layer pattern):**
Consumer reality: shoppers with specific situations (skin type, injury, use case) can't get guidance from the catalog.
Business outcome: those shoppers convert instead of leaving or calling support.
Personal payoff: depends on role — Head of Support sees ticket deflection, Head of eCommerce sees conversion win for QBR, CMO sees the in-store experience online.

"An AI assistant that knows your full catalog and every review, guides shoppers to the right product through conversation. Live in under 4 weeks on your current platform, no data prep, no migration. We build a working version on your catalog before the first call."

**Score: 56.** Strong denominator. Numerator needs one real metric from existing client to break out.

## Cluster 3 — Agencies

**Dream outcome:** When clients ask about AI, you have a deployable answer. Win pitches. New revenue stream without building anything.

**Perceived likelihood:** Free pilot on real client catalog. Named brands. 20-25% rev share.

**Speed:** Pilot demo in days. Live on client store in 4 weeks.

**Effort:** Agency picks client. We build free. No ML expertise. No maintenance.

**Applied example (give frame, three-layer pattern):**
Consumer reality: agency clients asking about AI, agency needs a deployable answer.
Business outcome: AI assistant they can deploy on client stores, new revenue stream.
Personal payoff: wins the next pitch with a live demo instead of slides. Stays the expert, not the commodity.

"An AI shopping assistant you can deploy on any Shopify Plus client in 4 weeks, no ML team. We build the first pilot free on a real client catalog. You sell it, we deliver it."

**Score: 126.** Near-zero denominator. Best close rate.

## Wedge — Review Summaries (foot-in-door offer)

**Dream outcome:** Every product page has published pros/cons from real reviews.

**Perceived likelihood:** One click, see output instantly.

**Speed:** Minutes.

**Effort:** Zero. No integration.

**Applied example:**
"Your customers read reviews to answer one question: is this right for me? One click turns every product's reviews into a published pros and cons summary. No integration, no dev time. See the output on your own products in minutes."

**Score: 200.** Modest dream but near-zero denominator.

## Offer Hierarchy (what opens the door)

1. Review Summaries = lead offer (gets inside the account)
2. Agency Partnership = best close rate (existential dream, zero effort)
3. AI Assistant = core revenue (needs proof)
4. GEO = upsell after relationship exists

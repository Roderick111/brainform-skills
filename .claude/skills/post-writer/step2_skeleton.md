# Step 2: Skeleton

Build the architecture of the post before writing a single line of prose. This step produces the blueprint: titles, structure, flow, and the reader's next action.

---

## 1. Title options

Generate 4-5 title options. Titles can be CAPS (LinkedIn allows this for hooks).

Every title must pass at least one of these tests:
- **Dramatic** — stakes are high, something is at risk
- **Unresolved** — creates an open loop the reader needs to close
- **Unexpected** — contradicts what the reader assumes to be true

Bad titles announce topics ("How AI is changing ecommerce"). Good titles create tension ("GOOGLE PAGE ONE BUT INVISIBLE TO AI").

Present titles as a numbered list. No explanation needed, they speak for themselves.

## 2. Post structure

Map the post as a skeleton using this formula:

**Hook** — the first 1-2 sentences. What stops the scroll. This is the single most important part of the post. If the hook is weak, nothing else matters. The hook should create an information gap: the reader knows enough to be curious but not enough to scroll past.

**Context** — 1-2 sentences max. Just enough for the reader to understand what you're talking about. Not a backstory. Not an introduction. The minimum viable setup.

**Insight** — the core of the post. What you learned, saw, did, or built. This is where the value lives. Be specific about what goes here: a finding, a pattern, a story, a number.

**CTA** — what happens next for the reader. Options:
- Link (to tool, article, resource)
- Invitation ("drop your URL in comments")
- Open question that invites discussion
- Nothing (sometimes the insight is the end)

Write the skeleton as bullet points describing what each section contains. Not prose. Just the argument in order.

## 3. Flow check

Read the skeleton top to bottom. Answer:
- Does each section earn the next? (Hook earns context, context earns insight, insight earns CTA)
- Is there a section that could be cut without losing the argument?
- Does the hook promise something the post delivers?
- Is there a logical jump between any two sections?

Flag problems. Suggest fixes.

## 4. Message and next action

State in one sentence: **what should the reader think or do after reading this post?**

If you can't answer this clearly, the post doesn't have a point yet. Go back to the skeleton.

---

## Output format

```
## Title options
1. ...
2. ...
3. ...
4. ...
5. ...

## Skeleton
- Hook: [what it says and why it stops the scroll]
- Context: [minimum setup needed]
- Insight: [the core value, specific]
- CTA: [what the reader does next]

## Flow check
[Pass / issues flagged]

## Message
[One sentence: what the reader walks away with]
```

---

## Rules

- No prose in this step. Architecture only.
- The user picks the title. Don't pick for them.
- If the idea from Step 1 doesn't have a clear hook, say so. Don't force it.
- Skeleton should be tight enough that two people reading it would write roughly similar posts.

Wait for user feedback before proceeding to Step 3.

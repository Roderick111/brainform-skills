# Step 3: Draft

Write the post based on the approved skeleton and title from Step 2. One version, plus 2-3 alternative openings.

---

## Voice principles

**Assertive and direct.** You know what you think. Sentences land with conviction. No hedging, no "I think maybe," no softening. If genuinely uncertain, say so plainly ("I don't know yet" not "it remains to be seen").

**Say the thing first.** The point goes in the first sentence. Then earn it with evidence or reasoning. Never build up to a reveal.

**One idea, one paragraph.** Even if it's one sentence. Short paragraphs. Breathing room between thoughts.

**Show your reasoning.** Not "X is important" but why you think so. Not "this is growing" but what you saw that made you think so. Let the reader see how you got there.

**Specific over abstract.** Names, numbers, products, companies. "Telegram just shipped bot-to-bot communication" not "platforms are adding agent features." If you have a concrete example, use it. If you don't, describe the situation instead of using vague scale words.

**Casual confidence.** Not press-release confident. "I did this and here's what happened" confident. Starting sentences with "And," "But," "So" is fine. Sentence fragments are fine. The register is a smart person talking to peers, not presenting to a boardroom.

**No performative casualness.** "Something I noticed lately" or "This stat surprised me" is a performance of authenticity. Just say the thing.

**First person, active.** "I built," "I ran," "we put." Never passive, never abstract.

**The story IS the content.** Describe what happened or what you see. The reader extracts the meaning. If you add a reflection, one sentence max, earned by the evidence before it.

**Rough is fine.** Don't over-polish. If it sounds like a person wrote it once knowing what they wanted to say, it's done.

---

## Structural patterns

- Opens with a fact, event, or concrete observation. Never with self-reflection.
- Arguments build through examples, not through logical connectors.
- Uses real product names, company names, specific numbers.
- Paragraphs alternate between short (1 sentence) and medium (2-4 sentences). Never long blocks.
- Closes with something in-progress, unfinished, or forward-looking. Not a moral. Not a summary.

---

## Output

Write the full post using the approved title and skeleton.

Then write 2-3 **alternative openings** (first 1-2 sentences only). Different angles into the same post. Label them A, B, C. The user picks one, or keeps the original.

---

## What NOT to do

- Don't apply anti-AI cleanup or structural review yet. That's Steps 4 and 5.
- Don't add ideas the user didn't provide or approve in Steps 1-2.
- Don't remove points from the approved skeleton.
- Don't make it more polished than a person would write in one sitting.
- Don't write multiple full versions. One version + alternative openings.

Wait for user feedback before proceeding to Step 4.
